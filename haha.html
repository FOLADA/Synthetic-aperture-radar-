Introduction to Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)

SAR is a remote sensing technology that uses radar to create high-resolution images of the Earth's surface.

How SAR Works

SAR sends out radio waves and measures the signals that bounce back to create images. It works day and night and in all weather conditions.

Applications of SAR

SAR is used in agriculture, forestry, disaster monitoring, and more. It can detect changes on the Earth's surface over time.

Conclusion

Synthetic Aperture Radar is a powerful tool for understanding and monitoring our planet's dynamic processes.
